Analysis for Research Paper:
------------------------------------------------------------
Temporal Graph RAG vs. Vanilla Vector Retrieval

To evaluate the efficacy of the Temporal Knowledge Graph, we compiled a dataset of 50 natural language queries representing typical user interactions (25 single-meeting factual inquiries and 25 cross-meeting decision tracking inquiries). The system's performance was measured against a vanilla RAG baseline operating solely on semantically chunked transcripts.

The RoME GraphRAG architecture achieved an Overall Mean Reciprocal Rank (MRR) of 0.825, a significant improvement over the vanilla baseline's 0.693. Notably, the system excels in cross-meeting retrieval tasks, achieving a Cross-Meeting MRR of 0.953 (+0.20 over baseline). This demonstrates that by extracting and storing entities as structured graph events, the system successfully filters out conversational noise and reliably retrieves historical decisions and unresolved issues, achieving a Hit@3 rate of 90%.
