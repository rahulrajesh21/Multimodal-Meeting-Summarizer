\begin{abstract}
Virtual meetings have become ubiquitous in modern organizational workflows, yet extracting actionable insights from lengthy recordings remains challenging. This paper presents \textbf{RoME} (\textit{Role-aware Multimodal Meeting Summarizer}), a system that fuses textual, audio, and visual modalities through a Transformer-based architecture to generate context-aware summaries. Our approach introduces three key contributions: (1) a Contextual Fusion Transformer that learns optimal modality weights through attention, (2) a 4-layer Temporal Graph Memory (TGM) architecture for persistent reasoning, and (3) an autonomous agentic orchestration loop with gradient-based online learning. Experimental evaluation demonstrates that RoME significantly outperforms unimodal baselines, achieving a 22\% improvement in longitudinal accuracy.
\end{abstract}

\noindent \textbf{Index Terms}---multi-modal fusion, meeting summarization, transformer, temporal graph memory, speech recognition, BERT embeddings, attention mechanism
